# Final Presentation Recommendation Plan


## Goal 1: Regional Secondary Ambulance Network

Consolidation of each municipality's secondary ambulances into an integrated countywide network with X number of regional ambulances in X locations that report to secondary calls only when the first ambulance in any municipality has the first ambulance on a call.

Each call that cannot be completed by the primary ambulance will automatically be sent to the network of secondary ambulances, stationed at optimal locations around the county to minimize response time.

### Current Operations
Most municipalities staff their last ambulance using part time labor, leading to a huge total labor force of highly underutilized part time workers. Each secondary ambulance is expensive to maintain and is used sparingly, only when the primary is already out on a call.

### Major Sub-Goals

#### 1. Increase utilization of the secondary ambulances (up from 10-15% estimated)
- Each municipality will then rely on the regional ambulance nearest to it using SOME ALGORITHM (response time? expected emergency level?) for additional calls after the first is utilized
- Each regional ambulance can supply services for many municipalities at once, especially the small rural municipalities

#### 2. Reduce total labor force for EMS
- Transition from many part time to fewer full time EMS workers (funded through Levy?) for the county wide ambulances to each have a full-time staff
- Reduce the amount of on call part time staff at any given time for each municipality

#### 3. Reduced total ambulances needed
- As a function of goal #1, use each ambulance more
  - One implication may be faster depreciation of the secondary ambulances, which have already been in use for ~10 years in most towns
- Eliminate the need for municipalities to take care and maintain a secondary ambulance

#### 4. (Optional) Investigate secondary ambulance communication workflows between municipalities
- Poor workflow in place for responding to calls when the primary ambulance is at a call
- Improve the response time for the secondary ambulance and determine if the call should be answered by the main or neighboring municipality

### Tasks to Complete
1. Determine how many regional ambulances we need, targeting same service levels as current
   - Shifting requirements for peak call volume
2. Where to place them? Many in one location? Spread out around the county?
3. Financial implications of staffing
4. Financial implications of changed utilization of ambulances
5. Impact on current operation processes for each municipality
6. Define call pickup structure (how will those calls be sent to each secondary ambulance, how do we decide which one takes the call? What signaling can they use?)

## Goal 2: Investigate Peak Staffing Requirements

Investigate labor operations of each municipality and determine ways that staffing and labor allocation can be improved.

- Investigate peak call hours throughout the day and days of the week and see if staffing can be changed.
- Determine if the county was to provide a paid EMT, paramedic, etc, for EMS operations, what time of the day would this employee be best utilized.
  - Determine variability in call types and staffing requirements for the breakdown
