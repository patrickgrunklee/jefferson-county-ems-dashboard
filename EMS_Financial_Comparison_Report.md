# EMS Financial Comparison Report
## Jefferson County vs. Portage County vs. Bayfield County
### ISyE 450 Senior Design Project - Jefferson County EMS Study

---

## Executive Summary

Jefferson County's EMS system operates as a **fully fragmented municipal model** with 10+ independent providers, each funded by individual municipal levies. This contrasts sharply with Portage County's **hybrid county-contracted model** ($4M+ annual budget) and Bayfield County's **county-supported model** ($458K, rapidly scaling). The core financial challenge: Jefferson County municipalities are at or near their individual levy caps, while state law provides a **levy limit exemption for countywide EMS systems** that neither individual municipalities nor the county is currently using.

### 2.3 Payment Model Comparison

| Model | Used By | Pros | Cons |
|-------|---------|------|------|
| **Per Capita** | Jefferson, Fort Atkinson, Lake Mills, Waterloo, Watertown | Simple, predictable | Doesn't account for property values or call volume |
| **Equalized Value** | Johnson Creek (JCFD) | Proportional to tax base | Can fluctuate significantly year-to-year |
| **Fixed + CPI** | Edgerton FPD | Stable, predictable growth | May not keep pace with actual costs |
| **Formula (blended)** | Ixonia | Balances calls, population, and value | More complex to calculate |
| **County Levy** | Portage, Bayfield | Unified, exempt from municipal levy caps | Requires political will, governance structure |

## 6. Levy Limit Analysis

### The Core Problem
Jefferson County municipalities are individually at or near their levy caps. EMS costs compete with other municipal services for limited levy room. There is currently no countywide coordination or funding.

### The Opportunity
**Wis. Stat. § 66.0602(3)(e)6** provides that the levy limit **does NOT apply** to "the amount that a county levies in that year for a countywide emergency medical system." This is the same exemption used by Portage and Bayfield counties.

### How Others Have Done It

**Portage County**: Full county EMS levy funding $4M+ through contracts with 3 municipal fire departments. EMS Division in Sheriff's Office with dedicated coordinator (since 2009).

**Bayfield County**: Established countywide EMS in 2023. Rapid scaling: $185K (2024) to $458K (2025). Focused on stabilizing volunteer agencies and adding county-level coordination.

**Lafayette County**: $5/capita countywide levy distributed to municipalities by population. Created EMS Advisory Subcommittee. 5-year agreement term. Note: faced legal challenges from some municipalities.
