# Jefferson County EMS -- Secondary Network Replacement Summary

## Concept
Each municipality **keeps its primary (first-out) ambulance**. All 10 existing
secondary/backup ambulances are **replaced** by 10 county-wide ALS units stationed at
optimal locations. These units respond anywhere in the county when a primary is already committed.

## Fleet Impact
- **Primary ambulances retained:** 10 (unchanged, one per municipality)
- **Secondary ambulances retired:** 10 distributed units
- **Replacement county-wide units:** 10 ALS ambulances at optimal locations
- **Net reduction:** 0 ambulances (0% of secondary fleet)
- **Total fleet:** 10 primary + 10 secondary = 20 (was 20)

## How It Works
1. A 911 call arrives -- the local primary ambulance responds as usual
2. If the primary is already on a call, the nearest available county-wide secondary unit is dispatched
3. Secondary units are not restricted to municipal boundaries -- they serve the entire county
4. This eliminates the current pattern where some departments have idle backup ambulances
   while others rely heavily on mutual aid

## Key Advantage
Strategic placement means 10 county-wide units achieve comparable or better coverage
than 10 distributed units, because they are positioned to minimize
response time across the entire county rather than being anchored to individual municipalities.
